Video Games: Distraction or Gold Mine?

by Kevin Yan

In today’s world, nearly 40% of the population plays video games, especially teenagers such as myself. The reason for this is that our advanced technological tools have allowed us to develop highly entertaining video games of all types. However, as you probably already know, video games are highly distracting and addictive because playing video games releases dopamine into the brain through the experience of achievement or success, which leaves us feeling good and craving for more. If you are an avid gamer such as myself, you probably have been told by your parents hundreds of times of the negative effects of video games, and you have had little to disprove them. Although your parents may be partly correct, there are also numerous reasons that support playing and exploring video games, which is what you are going to learn through this article. 

Photo by JESHOOTS.COM on Unsplash

Photo by Florian Olivo on Unsplash

Firstly, the eSports industry is a huge and rapidly growing industry, earning almost one billion dollars this year. Esports stands for electronic sports, also known as professional gaming, where tournaments are held on multiplayer video game platforms. Numerous games are included in the esports industry, such as League of Legends, Overwatch, and CS:GO, and I am sure that you have seen or at least heard of some of these titles. Working towards becoming a professional player is a very real and glamorous career path to explore, with top players earning millions of dollars and becoming extremely famous. In addition, being a professional gamer means that you are free to play video games as much as you want, and being addicted doesn’t even matter, since it is your everyday job. If you become a successful professional gamer, your parents can never tell you to stop playing, because you would have the money and proof to tell them off! What’s even better, you can start at any age. Last year, 17-year-old Kyle Giersdorf won the Fortnite World Cup, earning $3 million dollars. 

This all sounds really fun and exciting, but there are numerous risks and downsides to gaming. First of all, the eSports industry is very similar to regular competitive sports, such as basketball, where talent and practice, among various other skills, are absolutely crucial to be successful. Countless people try to become professional gamers but only a select few can. A person has to have some degree of talent and be very dedicated to a certain game. This can become extremely tedious and discouraging, as repetitive practice is required to be successful. Even after you get skilled at the game, you still need countless other skills such as leadership and teamwork to become better than everyone else with the same goal. With all that being said, if you really love the game and want to become a professional, you should try to practice and see how you like that lifestyle. The truth is, not everyone has the right skills to become a professional gamer, but if you really love the game and dedicate yourself to it, I believe that you can make it!

Photo by Florian Olivo on Unsplash

Secondly, a safer, more tangible option is game development. The gaming industry is one of the biggest industries in the entire world, earning higher revenues than the movies, television, and music industries combined! Video game developers earn up to $90 000 per year, and playing video games is a crucial part of this job. Video game developers constantly play video games to test them out or to gain inspiration from other games. For example, a game that I love is League of Legends, created by Riot Games Inc. This company earns billions of dollars every year, and its employees constantly play the game to test out features and explore possible changes. However, even though this job sounds very fun, there is way more to it than purely playing video games. Programming skills and design skills are also crucial in order to pursue a career in game development. Therefore, I recommend you to take a course on game development first, such as the freely offered courses on Unity, a game development platform, and see if aspects of game development interest you. On the other hand, game development can also just be a hobby because it is really exciting to create your own game. You can use your imagination and create whatever you want, then publish it for your friends and family to play. Wouldn’t that be awesome?

Photo by Scott Graham on Unsplash

In conclusion, video games are not just an evil distraction, but an aspect of entertainment that can lead to rewarding, high-paying jobs. Next time your parents come into your room and stop you from playing, show them how much game developers or professional players make, and they might just let you keep going. Follow your dreams and never give up!

Buying What You Want (Without Begging Your Parents)

by Kevin Yan

Image Credit: 金 运

Everyone in the world has something that they want, and most of the time, it is something that can be bought with money. For some older folks, it might be a house or a car, but in the case of a 13-year-old me, it was simply a video game. Of course, when I was that age, my only source of income was a thin stream of allowance. Therefore, my first instinct was to ask my parents to buy it for me, and I’m sure many of us know all too well what happens after that. In my case, after working on a well-grammared, professionally structured speech for hours, containing arguments, facts, and articles, my mom responded with a simple “no.” I had almost given up on trying to become the world’s biggest Twitch streamer when I realized that I was old enough to get a part-time job. It was exciting and rewarding, and I was finally able to buy myself what I thought was the greatest thing in the world, Minecraft. I got what I wanted without having to pester my parents or beg my friends for money, and it felt great. 

Image Credit: ffstudios

Part-time jobs are a very common way to earn some pocket money as a full-time student. Besides that, if you are starting to worry about university applications, it is a great way to show them your experience and expertise. Now let’s dive into the numerous part-time jobs you can get. 

Image Credit: Scott Graham

First of all, if you have something that you are good at or that you are interested in, try to get a part-time job that relates to it. This way, you will have fun while working. For example, if you are great at swimming, lifeguarding is one of the most rewarding jobs out there. Tutoring in any subject is also a great way to give back to the community while earning some pocket money. You would simply be doing things that you already know how to do and feeling good about it. Another great option, if you get lucky, might be something like a video game instructor, where people pay you to play video games. Wouldn’t that be amazing? Having a special skill or a specific interest that relates to an actual job is not a privilege that many of us have, but the good news is, you don’t need it. For example, a job that I once had was delivering newspapers. It pays very well and requires no knowledge or experience to start out! Another great option is to be a waiter at your favorite restaurant. You can learn how to make food and you might even see your friends there.

Image Credit: Nathan Dumlao

Another factor you want to consider is the balance and allocation of time. You probably do not want a job that interferes with your homework time, or in my case, video game time. The job should not be greatly affecting your ability to get good grades in school or enjoy yourself. Even though you can usually choose how much time you wish to work, most of the time, a part-time job requires serious commitment, often up to five days a week. This is where you ask yourself how greatly you want the rewards and where you stay determined when working at your part-time job. However, there are still jobs that do not require too much time. For example, delivering newspapers only requires a few hours per week, and something like mowing lawns can be done according to your schedule. 

Finally, part-time jobs come with responsibilities, which can be daunting. It requires you to make a commitment and deliver results. However, part-time jobs are already significantly less challenging compared to the competitive full-time job market, and working in these jobs can help you develop skills and experience that will help you substantially in the future.
